Introduction

The intention of this section is not to give a design analysis on computer monitors, but to address the problem that is created when shopping for monitors. Having worked as a Tech Sales Supervisor at Staples for some time, I spent a lot of time trying to guide customers who would often seem to be teetering with anxiety having to think about choosing the perfect monitor out of a wall of fifteen bright screened monitors. If you aren’t someone looking for the most expensive and sleekest looking monitor, I would have to spend a good chunk of time explaining all the specifications and differences between our monitors to customers who didn’t have a strong tech background. My plan of attack when selling these monitors to customers, was to figure out the purpose a monitor would serve to them and highlight the key features and design of a monitor that closely matched their needs. Here were some questions I would ask to get them started:

Example 1

“Are you looking for something cheaper or for more generic use? This could be for school, work, or general – everyday use.”

Example 2

“Do you have limited space to fit the monitor and need something smaller, or were you looking for something with a larger display?”

Example 3

“Are you looking for a nicer (more expensive) monitor that looks crisp, maybe has a sleek design, or is primarily used for entertainment purposes?”

Example 4

“How is the lighting in the area you plan to put the monitor? Do you get a lot of direct/indirect sunlight or glare?”

End Use Cases

All these questions and more can help narrow down the features that aren’t always thought of when purchasing a monitor. For example, maybe your monitor was going into your office room where you would get a lot of glare off a monitor that had a glossy screen, so you know you should look for a monitor with a matte film on the screen. If you are gaming, you would have a much better experience on something that had a higher refresh rate or higher resolution. If you have a small desk space, you should make sure you choose a monitor that would fit and has a small base.


In our next section, we are going to discuss these key features and designs of monitors so that you can start narrowing down which monitor you should be looking for.
